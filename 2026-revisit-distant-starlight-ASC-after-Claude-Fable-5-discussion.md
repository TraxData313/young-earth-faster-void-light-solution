# Distant Starlight and a Young Universe — the Strongest Honest Case

> **2026 revisit.** This document supersedes my earlier fast-light-in-voids proposal. It was written in July 2026 after a long review discussion with Claude (Fable 5, Anthropic), which convinced me the variable-c route is empirically closed (see §2.2) and that the honest strongholds are the Anisotropic Synchrony Convention and gravitational time dilation. I keep the original write-up for the record — following the evidence where it leads is the whole point.

**TL;DR:** If the universe is ~6,000 years old (plain Bible reading), how do we see galaxies billions of light-years away? This repo presents what I believe is the most defensible answer: the **Anisotropic Synchrony Convention (ASC)**, backed by a genuinely open question in physics — the one-way speed of light — with **gravitational time dilation** models as a physical alternative. It also lists the popular answers that *don't* work and why, and the real open problems of standard cosmology, honestly framed.

---

## Epistemic framing (read this first)

I am not claiming to *prove* a young universe. My starting point is faith in God, who — being omnipotent — needs no mechanism at all. But as someone trained in physics and mathematics, I want to know whether a young universe can be held *coherently*, without contradicting observations or requiring God to deceive us. The claim of this repo is modest:

> A young universe with visible distant starlight is observationally coherent, and the strongest route to it is not new physics, but an under-appreciated *conventional freedom* that standard physics itself acknowledges.

## 1. The problem, stated fairly

Light from the Andromeda galaxy has to cross ~2.5 million light-years; light from JWST's deepest fields crosses billions. At c = 3×10⁸ m/s, that seems to require millions to billions of years of travel time — far more than ~6,000 years. This is the **distant starlight problem**, and it deserves a serious answer, not a hand-wave.

## 2. Popular solutions that fail (and why I reject them)

Intellectual honesty requires clearing the field first.

### 2.1 "Light was created in transit"

God created the universe with light already en route, so we see stars immediately. **Fatal flaw:** the light arriving today carries detailed *history* — supernova SN 1987A showed us a star exploding, complete with a neutrino burst and expanding debris rings. If that light was created in transit, we watched a movie of an event that never happened, starring a star that never existed. That makes God the author of systematic deception, which contradicts His character (Titus 1:2). This is the old *Omphalos* problem (Gosse, 1857), and it is theologically unacceptable.

### 2.2 "Light travels faster in deep space" (c-decay and variable-c models)

The idea that c was faster in the past (Setterfield, 1981) or is faster in low-density voids is empirically closed off. I examined this route carefully before abandoning it:

- **SN 1987A (168,000 ly away):** neutrinos and photons arrived within hours of each other, exactly as core-collapse models predict. Any significant light speed-up along the path would have separated them by centuries or more.
- **Light echoes** (SN 1987A's rings, V838 Monocerotis): we directly watch light fronts sweep across interstellar gas at speed c, *in deep space*. The one-way "never measured out there" claim fails for the two-way/echo geometry.
- **GW170817:** gravitational waves and gamma rays from a neutron-star merger 130 million ly away arrived 1.7 seconds apart — light speed matches gravity's speed to ~15 decimal places over the whole path.
- **Quasar spectra:** c enters the fine-structure constant α = e²/4πε₀ħc. If c (via ε₀, μ₀) differed out there, atomic spectral lines would differ. Observed: |Δα/α| < 10⁻⁵ out to billions of light-years.
- **The threshold dilemma:** if the speed-up kicks in at interstellar densities, it's refuted by radar ranging and spacecraft telemetry (Voyager, 160+ AU, light travel at c through widely varying densities). If it only kicks in at void densities, it doesn't help — the Milky Way alone is 100,000 ly across and is not a void, so the starlight problem survives *inside our own galaxy*.

Notably, the major young-earth organizations (CMI, AiG) have themselves abandoned c-decay for these reasons. Following the evidence, so do I.

## 3. The proposed solution: the Anisotropic Synchrony Convention (ASC)

### 3.1 The genuinely open question in physics

Here is a fact that surprises most people, yet is standard, mainstream physics: **the one-way speed of light has never been measured, and cannot be measured even in principle.**

Every measurement of c is *two-way*: light goes to a mirror and comes back, and we divide the round trip by the total time. To measure the *one-way* speed, you need two clocks at two locations, synchronized in advance — but every synchronization procedure already assumes a one-way speed. The circle cannot be broken. Einstein said it plainly in his 1905 paper: that light takes the same time in each direction is "*a stipulation which I can make of my own freewill*" — a **convention**, not a measurement.

This is the *conventionality of simultaneity*, developed by Reichenbach and Grünbaum and still defended in the literature (see Anderson, Vetharaniam & Stedman, *Phys. Rep.* 295, 1998). Any assignment where the round-trip average equals c is empirically equivalent: light could travel at c/2 outward and instantaneously inward, and **no experiment could ever detect the difference**.

### 3.2 ASC applied to Genesis

Jason Lisle's proposal (*Anisotropic Synchrony Convention — A Solution to the Distant Starlight Problem*, Answers Research Journal 3, 2010) is to take the convention in which **light travels instantaneously toward the observer**. Under ASC:

- Light from stars made on Day 4 reaches Earth on Day 4. No travel-time problem exists, by construction.
- Every astronomical event is timestamped at the moment we see it — which is arguably the natural convention of the biblical authors and of ordinary human observation ("when you see it, it happens").
- **No observation is contradicted**, because ASC is empirically equivalent to the standard (Einstein) convention. This is not a loophole I am exploiting; it is the same conventional freedom every relativist acknowledges.

### 3.3 What ASC costs — full disclosure

I state the price openly, because an argument that hides its costs is worthless:

- ASC is a **coordinate convention, not new physics**. It doesn't make light "really" fast; it says the question "how fast is one-way light *really*?" has no observer-independent answer.
- Consequently ASC is **unfalsifiable** — which means it cannot serve as *evidence* for a young universe, only as a demonstration of *coherence*. It removes the starlight defeater; it does not positively date the cosmos. For my purpose — showing the faith position is not in contradiction with observation — that is exactly what is needed.
- Under ASC the universe still contains processes (stellar physics, galaxy collisions) that look like long histories under the standard convention. ASC answers the *light travel* question specifically, not every age question (see §5).

## 4. The physical alternative: gravitational time dilation

For those who want a *physical* (falsifiable) model rather than a convention, general relativity offers a real mechanism: **clocks tick at different rates in different gravitational potentials and expansion histories**. This is textbook physics (GPS satellites correct for it daily).

- **Humphreys' white-hole cosmology** (*Starlight and Time*, 1994): if the universe is bounded with a center, Earth near the center could experience ~6,000 years of proper time while distant regions experience billions of years.
- **Hartnett's model** (*Starlight, Time and the New Physics*, 2007): builds on Carmeli's cosmological relativity toward the same end.

These models use real GR and are in-principle testable; they also have known quantitative difficulties (the dilation factors required are hard to achieve for nearby objects like Andromeda). I list them as the serious *physical* research program, with ASC as the *conventional* baseline that already secures coherence.

## 5. Honest limits

A credible repo must say what its solution does **not** solve. ASC and time dilation address **light travel time only**. Independent age arguments — radiometric dating, the cosmic microwave background's blackbody spectrum, ice cores, geology — are separate discussions requiring separate treatment, and pretending starlight was the only issue would be dishonest. This repo deliberately scopes to starlight.

Also worth knowing: within Christian tradition itself, reading the Genesis days as non-calendar days is not a modern retreat before science — Augustine argued it in *De Genesi ad litteram* (~415 AD), centuries before geology existed. I hold the young-earth reading by conviction, but the alternative has old ecclesial credentials, and charity toward believers who hold it is warranted.

## 6. Meanwhile, the standard model is not a finished edifice

None of the following proves a young universe — that would be the same fallacy in reverse. But it shows that ΛCDM cosmology is a working model with real open wounds, not a closed case that renders all alternatives unthinkable:

| Open problem | Status |
|---|---|
| **Hubble tension** | Local measurements give H₀ ≈ 73, CMB gives ≈ 67 km/s/Mpc — a >5σ discrepancy, unresolved for a decade |
| **JWST "impossible early galaxies"** | Massive, mature galaxies at z > 10, earlier than galaxy-formation models comfortably allow (Labbé et al. 2023; the "little red dots") |
| **Early supermassive black holes** | Billion-solar-mass quasars < 700 Myr after the Big Bang strain known growth rates |
| **Lithium problem** | Big Bang nucleosynthesis predicts ~3× more ⁷Li than old stars show |
| **Cosmological constant problem** | Vacuum energy predicted ~120 orders of magnitude above the observed value |
| **Dark matter** | 40+ years of direct-detection null results (LZ, XENONnT), while MOND fits galaxy rotation curves suspiciously well |
| **Evolving dark energy?** | DESI (2024–25) data prefer time-varying dark energy over a constant Λ |
| **CMB anomalies** | Hemispheric asymmetry, the Cold Spot, low-multipole alignments |

The right conclusion is humility on all sides: cosmology is hard, models change, and the person who says "the science is finished" is not describing science.

## 7. Summary

1. The distant starlight problem is real and deserves better than the traditional quick fixes — created-in-transit fails theologically, fast-light/c-decay fails empirically.
2. The one-way speed of light is genuinely conventional — this is standard relativity, not fringe physics.
3. Under the Anisotropic Synchrony Convention, distant starlight reaches a young Earth immediately, contradicting no observation, because no observation can distinguish synchrony conventions.
4. Gravitational time dilation models (Humphreys, Hartnett) offer a physical, falsifiable alternative research program.
5. This establishes **coherence**, not proof — which is the honest ambition of a position that ultimately rests on faith in God's word and power.
